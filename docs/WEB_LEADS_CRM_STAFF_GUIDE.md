# Innovex Web Leads CRM - Staff Operating Guide

## 1. Purpose

The Web Leads CRM is a separate workspace for website, SEO, and digital-service prospects generated by an external outreach agency.

It is not a calling system. Agents make calls using their own agency tools and only record actionable responses in Innovex.

Record a prospect when the organisation:

- requests information by email;
- asks for a follow-up;
- shows interest in an Innovex service;
- requests a meeting; or
- is ready to be submitted as a qualified lead.

Do not record every unanswered call, busy number, or unsuccessful contact attempt.

## 2. Access roles

### External Agent

Can access only the Web Leads CRM and only records created by their own account.

Can:

- add actionable prospects;
- maintain their own prospect details;
- add manual interaction notes;
- schedule, complete, reopen, and reschedule follow-ups;
- use active approved email templates;
- save and resume email drafts;
- submit qualification details;
- request meetings; and
- view their own reports and notifications.

Cannot access recruitment data, Talent Pool, Business Leads, calling features, finance, training, admin users, settings, audit logs, other agents' prospects, or email credentials.

### Web Leads Sales Manager

Can access all Web Leads CRM prospect records and management tools.

Can:

- review all agent activity;
- accept, reject, reopen, or request more information;
- assign leads and meetings to internal staff;
- edit meeting times and confirm meetings;
- add private internal notes;
- merge duplicate prospect records;
- manage approved email templates; and
- review team reports.

Cannot access CRM category settings unless separately authorised as an Innovex owner/admin.

### Super Admin / Admin

Has full portal access, including Web Leads CRM settings, categories, team permissions, and audit records.

## 3. Sidebar pages and why they exist

### CRM Dashboard

Purpose: a quick operational view of prospect volume, email requests, follow-ups, qualified leads, meetings, and outcomes.

Managers can filter by period, agent, category, status, and service. The agent summary helps managers identify coaching needs without opening every record.

### Add Prospect

Purpose: capture the first useful response quickly and consistently.

Required information protects lead quality. The system automatically records the logged-in user, creation time, last editor, and update time.

Before saving, the CRM checks telephone, email, website, and business name/postcode for possible duplicates.

### My Prospects

Purpose: search and manage actionable prospect records.

External agents see only their own records. Managers see all records and can filter by agent. Date filters refer to the prospect creation date.

Use Edit details when contact or business information needs correction. External-agent editing is disabled while a submitted qualification is locked for manager review.

### Email Requests

Purpose: focus on prospects currently waiting for information.

Staff must choose an approved template and an authorised sender mailbox. SMTP credentials remain on the server and are never shown in the browser.

Saved drafts can be resumed from Email History. Sent and failed messages remain attached to the prospect record and timeline.

### Follow-Ups

Purpose: prevent interested prospects from being forgotten.

The queue is generated from the complete database, not only the records visible on the current prospect page.

Views:

- Due today: incomplete follow-ups scheduled for today;
- Upcoming: incomplete follow-ups after today;
- Overdue: incomplete follow-ups before today; and
- Completed: finished follow-ups.

Open the prospect to complete, reopen, or reschedule the action.

### Qualified Leads

Purpose: hand high-intent opportunities to Innovex in a consistent format.

The qualification form requires decision-maker details, direct contact information, business problem, required services, website condition, budget, timeline, preferred meeting time, contact method, and detailed notes.

After submission, the qualification is locked while a manager reviews it. If the manager requests more information, it unlocks with the previous answers retained so the agent can correct and resubmit it.

### Meeting Requests

Purpose: coordinate requested meetings without exposing internal calendars or communication credentials.

Agents submit the preferred time, meeting type, email, phone, and notes. Managers can edit the time, assign an internal representative, approve, confirm, or reject the request.

### Email Templates

Purpose: maintain consistent, approved Innovex messaging.

Agents can use only active templates. Managers can create, edit, activate, or deactivate templates. Template placeholders include `{{contactName}}` and `{{businessName}}`.

### Reports

Purpose: measure quality and follow-up discipline rather than raw call volume.

Reports include prospects, emails, follow-ups scheduled/completed/overdue, interested and qualified prospects, accept/reject results, meetings, wins, and conversion rate.

Agents see their own performance. Managers can filter across agents and date ranges.

### CRM Settings

Purpose: maintain the approved list of business categories.

Only Admin and Super Admin can add, rename, activate, or deactivate categories. Deactivation prevents new use but preserves historic prospect records.

## 4. Standard agent workflow

1. Contact the organisation outside the Innovex portal.
2. If the response is actionable, open Add Prospect.
3. Search mentally for obvious duplicates and enter accurate business/contact details.
4. Select the relevant service or services.
5. Choose the correct early-stage status.
6. Add a useful summary of what the prospect said.
7. Schedule a follow-up when required.
8. If information is requested, open the prospect and send an approved email.
9. Add interaction notes after meaningful contact.
10. Submit the mandatory qualification form only when genuine interest is confirmed.
11. Respond promptly if a manager requests more information.

## 5. Manager review workflow

1. Review the prospect record and full activity timeline.
2. Check for possible duplicates and merge only after confirming both records represent the same organisation/contact.
3. Review qualification completeness and lead quality.
4. Use Request information when specific evidence is missing.
5. Use Accept only when Innovex should take ownership of the sales process.
6. Assign an appropriate internal representative.
7. Add private notes for internal context that external agents must not see.
8. Review and confirm meeting requests.
9. Use Update pipeline stage to progress accepted opportunities through proposal, won, or lost statuses.

## 6. Status guidance

### Agent-stage statuses

- New Prospect: actionable record created but no next step completed.
- Email Requested: prospect asked Innovex to send information.
- Follow-Up Required: a future contact action is needed.
- Interested: prospect has shown meaningful interest.
- Meeting Requested: prospect requested a discussion.
- Existing Supplier: prospect already uses another provider.
- Not Interested: prospect declined current services.
- Do Not Contact: no further contact should be attempted.
- Duplicate: agent suspects an existing CRM record.

### Manager-stage statuses

- Under Review: qualification is awaiting an Innovex decision.
- Accepted by Innovex: lead passed review and entered internal sales handling.
- Rejected by Innovex: lead did not meet current qualification standards.
- Proposal Required: Innovex must prepare a proposal.
- Proposal Sent: proposal has been delivered.
- Meeting Booked: meeting request has been approved or confirmed.
- Information Sent: approved CRM email was sent successfully.
- Won: opportunity converted.
- Lost: opportunity did not convert.

## 7. Interaction notes

Interaction Note is manual record keeping only. It does not start a phone call, WhatsApp conversation, or email.

Write factual notes:

- who was contacted;
- the contact method;
- what the prospect said;
- services discussed;
- interest level;
- agreed next action; and
- any follow-up date.

Avoid assumptions, unnecessary personal data, or insulting/informal commentary.

## 8. Email rules

- Use only an assigned sender mailbox.
- Use an approved active template.
- Confirm recipient and CC addresses before sending.
- Personalise the message without changing the core offer inaccurately.
- Add an optional agent signature only if the template does not already include the Innovex sign-off.
- Save a draft when information is incomplete.
- Check Email History before sending again.
- Never place passwords, SMTP details, or confidential internal notes in an email.

## 9. Follow-up rules

- Every promised action needs a date and time.
- Use High or Urgent only when business timing genuinely requires it.
- Complete a follow-up only after the action has happened.
- Reschedule instead of completing an unfinished action.
- Review Due today and Overdue at the start and end of every shift.

## 10. Data protection and security

- Never share your login.
- External agents must not attempt to access another agent's records.
- Do not upload the original agency lead spreadsheet to this CRM.
- Do not copy or export internal Innovex data.
- Use Do Not Contact immediately when requested.
- Private manager notes are for internal Innovex use only.
- Report incorrect access or suspicious behaviour to the Super Admin.

## 11. Daily staff checklist

### Start of shift

- Read unread CRM notifications.
- Review Overdue follow-ups.
- Review Due today follow-ups.
- Check manager requests for more information.

### During shift

- Add only actionable prospects.
- Keep statuses and notes accurate.
- Send approved information promptly.
- Record agreed next actions.

### End of shift

- Complete or reschedule remaining follow-ups.
- Check that email requests were sent or saved as drafts.
- Submit any genuinely qualified lead.
- Confirm there are no unhandled urgent notifications.

## 12. Troubleshooting

- Access restricted: ask the Super Admin to review the account's explicit permissions.
- Sender mailbox unavailable: the mailbox has not been assigned to the user.
- Possible duplicate warning: do not guess; ask a manager to review and merge.
- Email failed: verify recipient, sender assignment, and email configuration; do not repeatedly click Send.
- Qualification form hidden: the submission is locked during manager review.
- More information requested: reopen the prospect; the previous qualification answers should be available for correction.
- Missing follow-up: verify the selected Due today, Upcoming, Overdue, or Completed view and any active filters.

## 13. Training sign-off

Before receiving live access, each staff member should demonstrate that they can:

- add a clean prospect record;
- identify a duplicate warning;
- add an interaction note;
- schedule and reschedule a follow-up;
- use an approved email template and save/resume a draft;
- submit a complete qualified lead;
- request a meeting; and
- explain which data they are not allowed to access or export.
